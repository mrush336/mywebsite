Stories From The Field by Matthew Rushford

The Fire

It was a cold and clear day in the mountains. I thought about not going, but decided that I would hike for a few short hours since I had not been in awhile. I kept most of my gear already packed and ready to go just because I like to be prepared. In my haversack I kept the essentials like a belt knife, a small carving knife, jute twine, a small metal cup, a shemagh, gloves, and a ferro rod. After stopping by the local store to get some bottles of water and a few snacks I made my way to the interstate. It was about a one hour drive to the trailhead and I felt very relaxed and calm. Upon arriving at the parking lot I donned my boots and threw my bag over my shoulder and off into the great forest I went. All was well and I made really good time hiking at a brisk pace. It was early afternoon and the forest was alive with the sound of birds and insects. I stopped for a break at one of my favorite spots where the view into the hills was unbroken for miles. It is always times like this that I feel the most alive. The world seems like a comfortable place and I feel at peace with nature. With the sun warming my face I laid against a tree and slept.
